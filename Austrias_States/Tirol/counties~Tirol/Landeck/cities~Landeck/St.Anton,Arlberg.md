## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Sankt Anton am Arlberg, commonly referred to as St Anton, 
> is a village and ski resort in the Austrian state of Tyrol. 
> 
> It lies in the Tyrolean Alps, 
> with aerial tramways and chairlifts up to 2,811 m (9,222 ft), 
> yielding a vertical drop of 1,507 m (4,944 ft). 
> It is also a popular summer resort among trekkers and mountaineers.
> 
> Skiing has a long history in St. Anton: 
> ski instructors from the area emigrated to the United States in the 1930s, 
> helping to popularise the sport. 
> 
> St. Anton was the host of the Alpine World Ski Championships in 2001, 
> and is frequently listed as one of the world's top skiing resorts
